Why Fertilization Is Often Guesswork

Farmers often fertilize without knowing what the soil actually needs.

What happens

  • No soil testing before fertilizing
  • Wrong type or wrong amount
  • Decisions based on habits

Results

  • Fertilizer waste + higher cost
  • Soil degradation over time
  • Lower yield and crop quality
üå± Why Soil Matters

Understanding N, P, and K

Plants need the right balance of nutrients to grow and produce healthy crops.

Key nutrients

  • N: growth and leaves
  • P: roots and early development
  • K: fruits and quality

Main idea

  • Different stages need different levels
  • Soil may be low in one nutrient
  • So fertilizer must match the deficiency

üåø Artificial Intelligence

Where AI Makes the Difference

Regression (amount)

Type selection

üçÄ Fertilizer Quantity

How We Calculate the Exact Amount

We use a weighted approach instead of only the biggest deficiency.

Steps

  • Compute deficiencies: defN, defP, defK
  • Apply weights by stage importance
  • Output a single amount number

Why weighted?

  • Uses all nutrient shortages
  • More realistic than ‚Äúmax only‚Äù
  • Improves stability of recommendations
üå± Fertilizer Type

Choosing the Right Fertilizer

Type depends on which nutrient is most missing.

Simple mapping

  • K deficit ‚Üí Potash-based fertilizer
  • N deficit ‚Üí Ammonium-based fertilizer
  • P deficit ‚Üí MAP (phosphorus)

Balanced option

  • If multiple deficits ‚Üí 20-20-20
  • Always show a short reason
  • Farmer-friendly output
